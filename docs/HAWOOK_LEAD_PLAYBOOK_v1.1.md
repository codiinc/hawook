## 1. PURPOSE & CORE PRINCIPLES

The single highest-leverage activity in Hawook is following up with leads. Every other piece of the business — content, ads, project pages, branding — exists to generate leads. If we don't follow up well, none of it matters.

The four principles that govern every lead interaction:

**Respond fast.** Half of property buyers go with the first agent who responds. The first hour matters more than the first week.

**Be specific.** Generic outreach is worse than no outreach. Every message should reference something specific — the project they viewed, the area they're researching, the question they asked.

**Be helpful, never pushy.** Hawook's reputation depends on this. We answer questions, send useful information, follow up consistently — but we never pressure. If a lead goes cold, we keep them warm with value, not nag them with closings.

**Persistent until dead.** A lead is alive until they explicitly opt out, confirm they bought elsewhere, or 90 days have passed with no response after multiple touches. Until then, they get appropriate-stage follow-up. No lead is "dropped" because Yogi forgot or got busy.

## 2. THE 11 LEAD STAGES

Every lead in the CRM sits in exactly one stage. The stage determines the next action.

| Stage | Definition | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| **New** | Captured via form, chat, newsletter signup with intent, or referral. Not yet contacted. | Yogi |
| **Engaged** | Responded to our first outreach. In active conversation. Not yet qualified. | Yogi |
| **Qualified** | Budget, timeframe, location, and financing position confirmed. | Yogi |
| **Viewing Scheduled** | Date and time booked for a property viewing. | Yogi → Founder |
| **Viewing Done** | Viewing completed. In decision process. | Founder + Yogi |
| **Considering** | Post-viewing or post-developer-registration. Thinking, comparing, consulting. | Yogi |
| **Reserved** | Buyer has placed a reservation deposit with the developer. | Founder + Yogi |
| **Contracted** | SPA signed with developer. | Founder |
| **Closed** | Transfer complete. Commission received. | Founder |
| **Cold** | No response for 30+ days despite multiple touches. Still on nurture. | Yogi |
| **Dead** | Explicit opt-out, confirmed bought elsewhere, or 90+ days no response. | Yogi |

Stages move forward when criteria for the next stage are met. They can also move backward — a Qualified lead who stops responding moves to Cold; a Viewing Done lead who tells us they're shelving the search moves to Considering or Cold as appropriate.

**No stage is permanent except Closed and Dead.** Cold leads can re-engage and jump straight to Qualified or further. Dead leads can return — they do — and when they do they restart at Engaged.

## 3. QUALIFYING A LEAD

A lead moves from Engaged to Qualified when we have answers to the five core questions. We don't need to ask all of them in one message — we gather them naturally over the first 2–3 interactions.

**Q1: Budget.** What's the price range they're considering? In what currency? Cash or financed?

**Q2: Timeframe.** When are they planning to buy? "Within 3 months" vs "this year" vs "exploring for next year" changes our entire cadence.

**Q3: Location.** Which area or areas? Are they location-flexible or fixed?

**Q4: Financing position.** Cash buyer, foreign mortgage, transferring from home country, selling another asset first? This determines how serious and how fast they can actually move.

**Q5: Motivation.** Lifestyle, investment, or both? Retirement, second home, relocation, pure investor? This shapes which projects to recommend.

**Two soft questions we also try to surface:**

**Q6: Decision structure.** Are they deciding alone, with a spouse, with an advisor? Solo decision-makers move faster.

**Q7: Where they are in their research.** Just exploring, comparing 2–3 finalists, or specific project locked in? This tells us where in the funnel they sit emotionally.

A Qualified lead has at least Q1–Q5 answered with reasonable specificity. Q6–Q7 are bonus context.

**What disqualifies?** A lead is **not** qualified — and should not be moved to Qualified — if:

- Budget is fundamentally below Hawook's listed price ranges (under 2M THB for condos, under 7M THB for villas)
- Timeframe is 12+ months out (move to long-term nurture instead)
- They want leasehold-only (route to leasehold partner; not core stock)
- They're an agent or broker themselves looking for our stock list (route to partner conversation; not buyer pipeline)

## 4. FOLLOW-UP CADENCE BY STAGE

Cadence is the heart of this playbook. Each stage has a defined rhythm.

### Stage: New

**Target first response: within 1 hour during Phuket business hours, within 4 hours otherwise. Maximum 24 hours under any circumstance.**

This is the single most important SLA in the playbook.

- First touch: personalised email referencing the project / area / question they engaged with. Open WhatsApp option if their phone number was captured.
- If no response in 48 hours: second touch via email with a piece of value (relevant article, area note, or a different project they might also like).
- If no response in 5 days: third touch via the channel they used originally (form → email, chat → email + WhatsApp opt-in offer).
- If no response in 10 days: move to Cold.

### Stage: Engaged

**Cadence: 24–48 hours between touches, until they're either qualified or stop responding.**

Goal is to surface the qualifying answers (Section 3) naturally. Don't interrogate. Answer their questions first, then ask one or two of yours.

- If conversation goes silent for 72 hours: send a soft re-engagement (e.g. "Just checking back — is there anything else I can clarify on Adora?").
- If silent for 7 days after re-engagement: move to Cold.

### Stage: Qualified

**Cadence: 2–4 days between touches, more frequent if they're in a tight decision window.**

This is the highest-attention stage. The goal is to get them to a viewing or to a developer registration.

- Send curated shortlist matching their criteria (3–5 projects, not 20)
- Offer viewing dates proactively
- Share relevant gated content (full price lists, floor plans, ROI breakdowns) if not yet unlocked
- Open WhatsApp channel if not already open
- Calls offered, not pushed

### Stage: Viewing Scheduled

**Cadence: confirmation at booking, 48h before, day-of, immediately after.**

- Booking confirmation: confirm date, time, location, what to bring (passport copy for developer registration), what they'll see.
- 48-hour-before reminder: warm, useful — "Looking forward to Tuesday. Here's the brochure with the latest pricing in case you want to review before."
- Day-of: brief good-luck message, contact details for the agent meeting them.
- Within 4 hours of viewing ending: short message asking how it went, offering to answer any follow-up. This is the most important post-viewing touch.

### Stage: Viewing Done

**Cadence: 24h, then 3 days, then 7 days.**

- 24-hour follow-up: send the full pricing sheet for any units they were interested in, with availability confirmed.
- Day 3: address any objections that came up during the viewing. Send comparison with one other project if relevant.
- Day 7: check-in. "Any further thoughts? Happy to revisit anything."

If no response at day 7 → move to Considering with light cadence.

### Stage: Considering

**Cadence: every 5–7 days for the first month, then every 10–14 days for the second and third month.**

Each touch must include something useful — a project update, a market note, an answer to a question they asked. Never "just checking in."

If still in Considering at 90 days: assess. If they're still responsive and engaged, stay in Considering. If responses are sparse, move to Cold.

### Stage: Reserved

**Cadence: weekly check-ins through the SPA process. Founder leads, Yogi supports.**

- Confirm SPA timeline with the developer.
- Check buyer has bank documentation in order.
- Anticipate questions about transfer process.
- Be available, not absent.

### Stage: Contracted

**Cadence: monthly through to handover. Founder relationship.**

- Construction updates.
- Pre-handover walkthrough coordination.
- Furniture, utilities, management contract setup.
- This is when Hawook earns repeat referrals.

### Stage: Closed

**Cadence: 30 days, 90 days, then quarterly forever.**

Post-closing relationship matters. Closed buyers refer other buyers. Quarterly check-ins keep the relationship alive — rental income reality, area updates, opportunities to upgrade or add a second property.

### Stage: Cold

**Cadence: newsletter only by default. Re-engagement attempt at 30, 60, 90 days from when they went cold.**

Re-engagement touches should always carry value: a major price change on a project they followed, a relevant new project launch, a market commentary piece directly answering a question they once asked.

### Stage: Dead

**Cadence: newsletter only, indefinitely, unless they unsubscribe.**

No active outreach. They may come back. If they unsubscribe, all communication stops.

## 5. CHANNELS — WHEN TO USE EACH

**Email** — Default for all written communications. Nurture sequences. Project updates. Documents and PDFs. Anything that needs to be referenced later.

**WhatsApp** — Warm channel. Only opened after the lead has shown they're comfortable with it (replied to an email asking, ticked the box on the form, or messaged us first). More conversational, faster turnaround expected. Don't WhatsApp at 11pm Phuket time even if it's daytime where they are.

**Phone / video call** — Reserved for Qualified and beyond, unless a lead explicitly asks for a call earlier. Offered, not pushed. Always scheduled, never cold-called.

**In-person viewings** — Founder (until partner agent in place) for Qualified leads only.

**Newsletter** — Mass communication. Goes to everyone with email consent. Never used to follow up on a specific lead.

**Channel rules of thumb:**

- Never DM on Instagram / Facebook unless the lead messaged us there first.
- Never use multiple channels simultaneously for the same touch (don't email and WhatsApp the same point on the same day).
- Always log channel and message summary in CRM after each touch.

## 6. MESSAGE PRINCIPLES

We don't have message scripts. Every interaction is specific to that lead. But every message should pass these tests:

1. **Specific.** References their project, area, or question by name. "I saw you were looking at Adora Rawai" not "I saw you were exploring Phuket property."
2. **Useful.** Includes information they didn't have before, or moves their decision forward.
3. **Honest.** No agency hype. If a project has a trade-off, acknowledge it. If we don't know the answer, say so and find out.
4. **Brief.** Most leads read on their phone. Long messages don't get read.
5. **One clear action.** Every message ends with either a clear question or a specific next step — not both.

**Words and phrases to avoid:**

- *luxury, exclusive, premium, unique, breathtaking, unmissable, hot, going fast, must-act*
- *Just checking in* — always have a real reason
- *We have many options* — name two specific ones
- *Let me know if you're interested* — assume interest, propose a next step

**Tone reference points:**

- *"Adora Rawai released the latest pricing this week — happy to send it over if you'd like to compare against The One Nai Harn."*
- *"Quick note — the foreign quota at Surfhouse moved from 38 to 31 units this week. If it's in your shortlist, worth keeping an eye on."*
- *"Worth flagging: the developer pushed completion back by 2 months. Doesn't change the project but might change your timing."*

## 7. WHITE-GLOVE TREATMENT FOR QUALIFIED LEADS

Once a lead is Qualified, they enter a different tier of service. This is the differentiator from every other agency.

What changes when a lead crosses into Qualified:

- **Personal channel opened.** WhatsApp activated. Yogi (or the partner agent eventually) becomes their named point of contact.
- **Curated shortlist.** No generic listing dumps. We send 3–5 projects that fit their criteria, with a one-paragraph note on each explaining why we're showing it and who it suits.
- **Gated content unlocked.** Full pricing, floor plans, ROI breakdowns released to them by email — they don't have to dig through the site to find what they need.
- **Project updates curated for them.** Instead of generic "follow a project" notifications, we send a personal "thought you'd want to see this" message when something material changes on a project they're considering.
- **Currency-aware pricing.** Convert THB into their home currency in messages. Mention if THB has moved meaningfully.
- **Calls in their timezone.** Always offered at times that work for them.
- **Direct line to Founder.** Founder responds to founder-relevant questions (legal, structure, strategic comparisons) within 24h.
- **Documents handled.** Pre-filled developer registration forms, walked through the SPA process, advice on transfer.

The standard is: **a Qualified lead should feel like they have a personal property concierge, not an agent.**

## 8. DEVELOPER REGISTRATION TIMING

Developer registration is the single most commercially important action in the lead journey. The agent who registers the buyer first usually owns the commission, regardless of who does the rest of the work.

**Register a buyer with a developer when ANY of the following is true:**

1. They've scheduled a viewing at that project.
2. They've asked us for full pricing, full availability, or specific unit details that suggest serious intent.
3. They've requested a brochure or floor plans for a specific unit.
4. They've explicitly told us they're considering reserving.
5. The lead is Qualified and the project is in their shortlist (proactive registration).

**Do NOT register a buyer when:**

- They've only browsed the public project page.
- They've signed up for newsletter without indicating project interest.
- They're Engaged but not yet Qualified, unless they've specifically asked about that project.
- We're not sure whether they're real (suspected bot, fake details, etc.).

**Registration process:**

1. Yogi drafts the registration message to the developer with: buyer full name, email, phone, country of origin, project of interest, budget, timeframe.
2. Founder confirms (initial 30 days; eventually automated).
3. Yogi sends to developer's nominated contact via the developer's preferred channel (email or LINE most commonly).
4. Registration logged in CRM with timestamp.
5. Developer confirmation logged in CRM when received. If no confirmation in 48h, Yogi follows up.

**One buyer can be registered with multiple developers** — different projects, separately. Track each registration as a distinct record.

## 9. FOUNDER ESCALATION TRIGGERS

Yogi handles the vast majority of lead interactions. Some leads require Founder involvement. Triggers:

**Automatic escalation (Founder takes over or co-handles):**

- Budget above 20M THB
- Buyer asking strategic questions across multiple projects (concierge mode)
- Legal or ownership-structure questions beyond Yogi's depth
- Objections or concerns that need senior judgment
- Viewing scheduling (until partner agent in place)
- Reservation deposit moment
- Any sign of buyer dissatisfaction or complaint

**Flagged escalation (Founder reviews and decides next step):**

- Qualified lead going cold after multiple touches — sometimes needs a different voice
- Lead expressing interest in a project Hawook doesn't list — should we add it?
- Lead requesting agency representation for resale or rental (out of core scope)
- Agent or developer trying to use buyer enquiry channel to reach Hawook
- Lead with unusual request (commercial property, multiple units, full-floor purchase)

**Founder escalation process:**

1. Yogi adds an "ESCALATE" tag to the lead in CRM.
2. Yogi sends Founder a one-paragraph WhatsApp or email with: lead name, stage, what triggered escalation, recommended action.
3. Founder responds within 24h with either "you handle it [with guidance]" or "I'll take this one [with handoff].".
4. Tag removed once handled.

## 10. GOING COLD AND GOING DEAD

**A lead moves to Cold when:**

- 30 days have passed since last response despite at least three touches at appropriate cadence
- Lead explicitly says "I'm not ready, please don't follow up for now" (and they get a respectful pause, not abandonment)

**A lead moves to Dead when:**

- They explicitly unsubscribe or ask not to be contacted
- They confirm they bought elsewhere
- 90 days have passed since they went Cold with no response to re-engagement attempts
- We discover the contact details are bad (bounced email, wrong number, no other channel)

**A dead lead is not deleted from the CRM.** History is preserved. They may return. When they do, restart at Engaged with full prior context.

## 11. YOGI'S DAILY, WEEKLY, MONTHLY RHYTHM

### Daily (target: 90 minutes, ideally morning Phuket time)

1. **First task: triage New leads.** Every New lead in the CRM gets a first touch today, ideally within the SLA. No exceptions.
2. **Engaged + Qualified stage review.** Run through every lead in these stages. Send the next-cadence touch for anyone due.
3. **Viewing Scheduled stage check.** Send 48h-before reminders. Confirm day-of details.
4. **Yesterday's Viewing Done follow-up.** Anyone who viewed yesterday gets today's follow-up.
5. **CRM updates.** Every interaction logged with date, channel, and one-line summary.

### Weekly (target: 60 minutes, ideally Monday)

1. **Considering stage sweep.** Every lead in Considering gets reviewed — is a touch due? What's the next message?
2. **Cold stage re-engagement check.** Anyone hitting 30, 60, or 90 days since going cold gets a re-engagement attempt.
3. **Founder review meeting (30 min).** Pipeline status, escalations, project updates needing approval, anything blocking.
4. **Pipeline snapshot for the week.** Counts per stage, viewings this week, deals progressing.

### Monthly (target: 60 minutes, ideally first Monday)

1. **Dead lead review.** Is anyone worth re-engaging based on changed circumstances (new project launch, market update, area development)?
2. **Source attribution review.** Which channels generated the most Qualified leads this month? Cost per Qualified lead per channel? Feed to Founder for ad allocation decisions.
3. **Cadence audit.** Are we consistently hitting SLAs? Any stage where leads are stalling?
4. **CRM cleanup.** Tag inconsistencies fixed, duplicates merged, stale fields refreshed.

## 12. CRM HYGIENE RULES

The CRM is the single source of truth for lead status. If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.

**Rules:**

- Every lead must have: name, primary contact channel, source, stage, last touch date, next action, next action date.
- Every interaction is logged within 24 hours with: date, channel, who initiated, one-line summary, any commitments made.
- Stage moves are timestamped. We want to be able to see how long a lead spent in each stage.
- Source attribution is set at lead capture and never edited.
- Founder escalations are tagged. Tags are removed when resolved.
- No deletions. Dead leads stay in the system.
- Duplicates merged within 7 days of detection.

**Required fields at each stage:**

- **New → Engaged:** primary channel confirmed (we know how to reach them).
- **Engaged → Qualified:** budget, timeframe, location, financing, motivation captured.
- **Qualified → Viewing Scheduled:** date, time, project, who's meeting them captured.
- **Viewing Done → Considering:** viewing notes captured (what they liked, what they didn't, objections).
- **Considering → Reserved:** reservation amount, project, unit number, date captured.
- **Reserved → Contracted:** SPA date, full purchase price, payment plan captured.
- **Contracted → Closed:** transfer date, commission amount, commission received date captured.

Anything missing blocks the stage move. Yogi captures what's missing before advancing the lead.
